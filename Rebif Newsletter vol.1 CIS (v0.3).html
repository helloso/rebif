Efficacy on CIS patient

Rebif ®는 위약 대비 2년간 McDonald MS로의 진행 위험을 51% 감소시켰습니다*1

Rebif ®는 CIS 환자의 McDonald MS Conversion을 늦추었습니다*1

STUDY DESIGN: A multicenter phase 3 study (REbif FLEXible dosing in early MS [REFLEX]) that included patients (aged 18‒50 years) with a single clinical event suggestive of MS, and at least two clinically silent T2 lesions on brain MRI. Participants were randomly assigned in a 1:1:1 ratio by use of a centralized interactive voice response system to receive the serum-free formulation of subcutaneous interferon beta-1a 44 μg three times a week or once a week (plus placebo twice a week for masking), or placebo three times a week for up to 24 months.

 

REFERENCES: 1. Comi G, et al. Lancet Neurol. 2012;11:33-41.
FOOTNOTES: CIS, Clinically Isolated Syndrome; MS, multiple sclerosis
* Rebif® three times a week (TIW)

Rebif ®를 조기에 시작한 경우, 늦게 시작한* 환자군보다 장기간 CDMS 진행 위험을 감소시켰습니다1

Rebif ®는 CIS 환자의 CDMS Conversion을 장기간 늦추었습니다*1

STUDY DESIGN: Patients who completed the 24-month double-blind REFLEX (REbif FLEXible dosing in early MS) study entered an extension (REFLEXION, REbif FLEXible dosing in early MS extensION): patients initially randomised to scIFN β-1a and not reaching clinically definite MS (clinically definite MS, CDMS (second attack or sustained Expanded Disability Status Scale (EDSS) score increase)) continued original treatment (three times weekly (tiw) or once weekly (qw)); placebo patients switched to tiw(DT); patients with CDMS switched to tiw. 402/517 (77.8%) REFLEX patients entered REFLEXION (DT, n=133; tiw, n=127; qw, n=142). The primary end point was time to CDMS conversion (defined in REFLEX) 2 from first randomisation to month 36.

 

REFERENCES: 1. Comi G, et al. J Neurol Neurosurg Psychiatry. 2016;0:1-10.
FOOTNOTES: CIS, Clinically Isolated Syndrome; CDMS, Clinically Definite Multiple Sclerosis
* REFLEX study의 위약군 중 2년간 CDMS로 진행하지 않은 환자에게 Rebif® 44 μg TIW 투여를 시작, delayed treatment 군으로 정의함

Rebif ®는 CIS 환자의 MRI 병변 발생을 감소시켰습니다*1

STUDY DESIGN: Patients who completed the 24-month double-blind REFLEX (REbif FLEXible dosing in early MS) study entered an extension (REFLEXION, REbif FLEXible dosing in early MS extensION): patients initially randomised to scIFN β-1a and not reaching clinically definite MS (clinically definite MS, CDMS (second attack or sustained Expanded Disability Status Scale (EDSS) score increase)) continued original treatment (three times weekly (tiw) or once weekly (qw)); placebo patients switched to tiw(DT); patients with CDMS switched to tiw. 402/517 (77.8%) REFLEX patients entered REFLEXION (DT, n=133; tiw, n=127; qw, n=142). The primary end point was time to CDMS conversion (defined in REFLEX) 2 from first randomisation to month 36.

REFERENCES: 1. Comi G, et al. J Neurol Neurosurg Psychiatry. 2016;0:1-10.
FOOTNOTES: CIS, Clinically Isolated Syndrome; MRI, magnetic resonance imaging
